**the curated, assembled, ready-to-run *content* layer** — "skills, packaged into a job you can
install in an afternoon."

> **Free à-la-carte skills vs. a curated, assembled, ready-to-run pack + setup guide.**
> ClawHub is genuinely free and genuinely good. But it's a parts bin: to run outbound you have

> tested to work together, delivered as one zip with a setup guide that tells you exactly what to

This framing survives a skeptic because it's true. It also positions us *alongside* ClawHub, not
against it — we send people to ClawHub for free skills (`free-lead-gen.md`), which is honest and
builds trust.

## Objection handling

**"Isn't ClawHub free? Why would I pay?"** (the big one)
> Yes — ClawHub is free, and you should use it. Clawmart isn't selling access to skills; it's
> selling curation and assembly. To make OpenClaw run outbound sales from the free registry, you
> have to figure out which skills you need, find them among thousands, vet their quality, install
> them in a sensible order, and connect them to your stack. A pack is that decision already made:
> six skills chosen to work together, in one download, with a setup guide. If your time is worth
> more than $39, that's the trade. And every pack shows one full skill free, so you can see the
> quality before you decide. 14-day refund if it's not worth it.

**"Can't I just write these myself?"**
> Absolutely — and if you enjoy that, you should. Skills are just folders with a SKILL.md; nothing
> is hidden. What you're buying is that someone already did it well: the trigger phrases, the
> step-by-step logic, the edge cases, the "what to configure" — written to OpenClaw's AgentSkills
> spec and organized into a coherent workflow. It's the difference between a recipe and an empty
> kitchen. Read the free sample skill in full on any pack page; if you look at it and think "I'd
> write this in an afternoon," then do — that's a completely fair call, and no hard feelings.

**"Are these tested against my exact setup?"**
> No, and we say so plainly. Packs are curated, ready-to-install instruction bundles built to the
> AgentSkills spec, with a setup guide — you adapt them to your channels and tools. They're not
> turnkey magic and they don't touch your data or your money. That honesty is why there's a
> 14-day, no-questions refund: install it, try it, and if it doesn't fit your stack, get your
> money back.

**"$39 — is it any good, or is it a cheap teaser?"**
> Each pack is six genuinely-useful skills, not filler, plus a real setup guide. Premium single
> skills on ClawHub's marketplace sell for $10–$200
> [[ClawHub skill economy]](https://medium.com/@0xmega/the-clawhub-skill-economy-how-builders-are-making-600-20-000-month-selling-ai-agents-0b56d4aede5e),
> so $39 for six curated ones that work together is deliberately at the accessible end. And you
> can read one of them free before buying. If the free sample doesn't convince you, don't buy.

**"Are you affiliated with OpenClaw?"**
> No. Clawmart is an independent storefront and is not affiliated with or endorsed by OpenClaw.
> We use the name nominatively to say what the packs are for, we don't use their logos, and we
> point you to their free registry ourselves.

**"Why not a subscription?"**
> Because a pack is a thing you install once, not a service you rent. Buy it, download it, own it.
> The bundle even includes future packs at no extra cost. If OpenClaw *hosting* is what you want
> to pay monthly for, that's a different product (e.g. OneClaw) — not us.

## Pricing rationale

- **$39 per pack — the impulse zone.** Below the ~$50 threshold where a solo operator needs to
  think hard or get approval. Cheap enough to try on the strength of one free sample skill +
  a refund; not so cheap it signals "junk." Anchored *under* the price of a couple of individual
  premium skills ($10–$200 each), so a six-skill curated pack reads as obvious value.
- **$99 All-Access — the anchor and the best deal.** Four packs bought separately = $156, so the
  bundle is ~36% off *and* includes every future pack. It makes any single $39 pack look
  reasonable by comparison and gives the "just give me everything" buyer a clean choice. This is
  the same tier structure OpenClaw pack-sellers already use on Gumroad ($49 / $99).
  [[Gumroad example]](https://numbpilled.gumroad.com/l/masterclaw)
- **One-time, not subscription.** Matches what the product actually is (a download) and removes
  the biggest objection to a low-priced tool (recurring charges for something you install once).
- **14-day refund on everything.** The honest counterweight to "not tested against your exact
  stack." It converts skeptics, it's cheap to honor for a digital product, and it's the right
  thing to offer for something you can't guarantee fits.
