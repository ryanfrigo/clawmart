## Objection handling

**"Can't I just ask ChatGPT myself?"** (the big one)
> You can, and you should try it once. But one question on one day isn't a measurement.
> Clawmart runs a fixed, versioned prompt set (~40 buyer-intent prompts) across three model
> families with three repeats each, because answers vary run to run and model to model. It
> separates search-grounded results (which you can influence in weeks) from
> memory-based results (which you can't directly control) — a distinction a single chat
> hides. Then it hands you the paste-ready fixes and the full transcript of every one of
> those ~360 calls, with model IDs and timestamps, so the result is reproducible instead of
> a screenshot you'll never trust next month. The value isn't "we asked ChatGPT for you" —
> it's the structure, the repeats, the evidence, and the artifacts.

**"This isn't how the real ChatGPT app answers me."**
> Correct, and we say so next to every number. We query the models via APIs; the consumer
> apps differ because of web search, memory, personalization, location, and routing. That's
> exactly why every claim links to the raw transcript with the model ID — it estimates
> model behavior, it isn't a recording of any real user's session. We'd rather be precise
> about what we measured than imply a wiretap.

**"Does any of this actually improve my visibility?"**
> AI visibility is a young field; the evidence for these practices is emerging, not proven,
> and we won't claim a guaranteed lift. What we do is label every fix with its mechanism
> (does it affect the search-grounded layer or the model's training?) and honest latency
> (weeks vs. slow-and-not-controllable). The fixes — crawlability, structured data,
> direct-answer copy — are mechanistically reasonable and cheap, and most of them also help
> ordinary SEO. You're buying a well-sampled baseline and a concrete, evidence-labeled
> to-do list, not a promise.

**"Why one-time instead of a subscription?"**
> Because most small teams don't need to watch a number daily — they need to fix it once
> and move on. If you later want continuous monitoring, that's a real (separate) job and
> there are good subscription tools for it. We'll add a monthly option only if enough people
> ask for it.

**"$49 feels cheap — is it real?"**
> The kit runs ~360 real LLM calls and generates five artifact types; our cost is a few
> dollars, so $49 is a fair one-time price, not a loss-leader trap. Guest checkout, 14-day
> no-questions refund, statement descriptor CLAWMART.CO. If generation fails or is late, the
> system flags an automatic refund.

**"You're not affiliated with OpenAI/Anthropic/Perplexity, so how can you claim anything?"**
> We don't claim affiliation — footer says so plainly. We query their public APIs, name
> them nominatively, and show the transcripts. The whole design is built so you don't have
> to trust us: check the receipts.

## The moat: honest methodology as the differentiator

Every competitor can query the same models. The defensible position isn't the sampling —
it's the **honesty stack**, and it's genuinely hard for a hype-driven competitor to copy
without undercutting their own marketing:

1. **Uncertainty on the label.** Wilson intervals, not fake-precise percentages. Tiers, not
   integers, at small sample sizes. We refuse to imply three-decimal certainty from a
   handful of prompts.
2. **Receipts on every claim.** Full timestamped transcripts with model IDs. "Check our
   work" is the product, not a nice-to-have.
3. **Mechanism + latency labels on every fix.** We tell you which fixes are slow and
   uncontrollable. A competitor selling "guaranteed #1 in AI search" structurally can't say
   this.
4. **A public, versioned methodology page.** Scoring formula, prompt-set changelog, model
   list, limitations, and the line: *"AI visibility optimization is a young field; evidence
   for these practices is emerging, not proven."*

The narrative in one breath, for any bio, cold email, or comment:
> In a category full of dashboards and guaranteed-lift claims, Clawmart is the one that
> shows its receipts: it measures your brand across the models behind ChatGPT, Claude, and
> Perplexity, ships the actual fixes, and links every number to a transcript — then tells
> you honestly which of those fixes might be slow to matter. Honesty is the product.
